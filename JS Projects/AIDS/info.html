Understanding HIV & AIDS

What is HIV/AIDS?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the immune system. If not treated, it can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome). HIV reduces the body's ability to fight infections and certain cancers.

How is HIV Transmitted?

HIV is transmitted through the exchange of certain body fluids such as blood, semen, vaginal fluids, and breast milk. Common ways include:

  • Unprotected sexual intercourse
  • Sharing needles or syringes
  • From mother to child during birth or breastfeeding
  • Contaminated blood transfusions

Symptoms and Stages

HIV infection progresses in three main stages:

  • Acute HIV: Flu-like symptoms within 2-4 weeks of infection.
  • Chronic HIV: Virus multiplies at low levels; may have no symptoms.
  • AIDS: Severe immune damage, opportunistic infections occur.

  • Avoid sharing razors or toothbrushes.

Treatment

There is no cure for HIV, but it can be controlled with Antiretroviral Therapy (ART). ART helps lower the viral load in the body, keeping the immune system healthy and reducing the risk of transmission.
